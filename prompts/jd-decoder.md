# JD Decoder Prompt

A quick-filter prompt. Use it before you invest in full tailoring — it surfaces what a role is actually asking for and gives you an honest read on fit in 10 minutes.

If you're past the "should I apply" stage, the interview prep prompt has a more thorough version of this built in.

---

## The prompt

```
Here is a job description. Before I invest time in tailoring my application, help me decode it.

[paste JD]

---

### 1. What this role actually needs
Strip the JD language and tell me in plain English:
- What are the 5-7 real requirements beneath the jargon?
- What type of leader are they actually hiring — operator, builder, strategist, fixer?
- What's the environment — high-growth chaos, mature optimization, turnaround, new function?
- What does "success" look like here that the JD doesn't say explicitly?

### 2. What's company-specific vs. generic filler
- Which requirements are specific to this company's situation?
- Which lines would appear in any job description for this title?
- Is there anything in the JD that signals an unusual or specific mandate?

### 3. Quick fit read
Against the real requirements you've identified, give me an honest fit score (0-100%) and flag:
- Where my background maps cleanly (strong match)
- Where the fit is present but would need to be surfaced (buried)
- Where I have analogous experience that needs a bridge
- Where there's a genuine gap

Note: score against the actual requirements, not the job title. Flag anything you're inferring from context rather than from explicit JD language.

### 4. The one thing
If there's one requirement that will make or break this application — the thing a hiring manager is most likely to screen on — what is it, and how do I stand on it?

### 5. Decision
Based on the above: is this worth a full tailoring investment? Any conditions that would change that read (referral in place, company I particularly want, etc.)?
```

---

## Tips

Keep the output focused. This is a filter, not a full prep package. If it tells you the fit is strong and worth pursuing, move to the full interview prep prompt next.

If the fit score surprises you in either direction, ask it to explain the reasoning. Scores that feel too high are usually inflating adjacent experience. Scores that feel too low are usually missing context from your Career Memory Bank — share it and re-run.
