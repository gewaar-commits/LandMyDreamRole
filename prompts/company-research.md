# Company Research Prompt

Before I tailor anything - resume, cover letter, interview pitch - I research the company; not a quick LinkedIn scroll, but a real working snapshot of what they do, where they're headed, what their systems landscape looks like, and what's actually happening in the business right now.

This prompt builds that snapshot. I run it once per company, early in the process, and refer back to it throughout.

---

## The prompt

```
I'm researching [COMPANY NAME] before applying for [ROLE TITLE]. Help me build a working snapshot I can use to tailor my application and prep for interviews.

Please cover:

**Business context**
- What does the company actually do, and who are their customers? (Cut through the marketing language.)
- Business model: how do they make money?
- Stage and scale: headcount, revenue range (public or estimated), growth trajectory
- Recent news: funding, acquisitions, layoffs, leadership changes, product launches - anything in the last 12-18 months worth knowing

**The role in context**
- Where does this function typically sit in companies like this? Who does it serve?
- What's the likely reason they're hiring for this now? (Scaling, backfill, new initiative, reorg?)
- What does success in this role probably look like at 6 months and 18 months?

**Tech and systems landscape**
- What's their known or likely tech stack for GTM/business systems? (CRM, marketing automation, data, CS tooling)
- Any public signals about their systems maturity - recent implementations, integrations, migrations?
- Are they building, buying, or consolidating right now?

**Culture and leadership signals**
- What's the leadership style signaled by their public comms, job postings, or press?
- Any signals about how they treat their systems and ops functions - strategic or support?
- Glassdoor themes worth noting (without over-indexing)

**Flags and open questions**
- Anything that would make this role harder than it looks on paper?
- What would I want to clarify in a first conversation?

Where you're uncertain, say so. I'd rather have honest gaps than confident guesses.
```

---

## How I use the output

The snapshot informs everything downstream - I check the tech stack against my experience before writing a single bullet, look at the business context before deciding on a positioning angle, and flag open questions for the recruiter screen.
